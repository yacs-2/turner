The Hobbit

J.R.R. Tolkien's The Hobbit is a very entertaining story about a young hobbit in the mythological land of Middle Earth. He goes on an adventure with several dwarves. Along the way he learns all about fellowship, bravery, and honor. The story fills the reader with inspiration, showing that no matter how small you might be, emotionally and/or physically, you can always overcome challenges with bravery.

Animal Farm

Animal Farm is a satirical allegorical novella, in the form of a beast fable, by George Orwell, first published in England on 17 August 1945. It tells the story of a group of anthropomorphic farm animals who rebel against their human farmer, hoping to create a society where the animals can be equal, free, and happy.

Frederick Douglas

Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglassis an autobiography detailing Douglass's experiences as an enslaved person in Maryland. It describes his brutal treatment, his yearning for freedom, and his eventual escape to the North. Douglass highlights the importance of education in his journey and critiques the dehumanizing system of slavery. His story became a powerful tool in the abolitionist movement, advocating for the emancipation of enslaved people and equal rights.
